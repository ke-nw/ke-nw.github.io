Minimalist Pigsgiving Frequently Questioned Answers

What's for dinner?

Cuban-Style Roast Whole Pig, served with tortillas, fixin's and mojo criollo (a citrus-garlic marinade/sauce).  Probably some Dirty Rice, but nowhere near enough to feed a crowd.

What should I bring?

There's no spreadsheet, no sign-up list.  One glorious year we had 5 potato salads, one of which turned out to be a Snickers Salad in disguise!.  Another year we wound up with a dessert tent.  Got time to make Grandma's 72-hour Prune Dip?  Go for it!  Got time to pick up a 172 oz. jug of Cheesy Spheres?  Children will sing of your kindness for generations!  Nothin' don't go with pig.

What time should I get there?

We're just happy you can come at all.

No really, what time?

There's a lot of pig to cook, somewhere between 40 and 60 pounds, so I can't give a precise time to show up.  We will try to aim for 4pm, but it has to rest for a bit, then get picked.  So, food's hot and ready between 4:30-6pm, cool but delicious between 6-7:30pm, and violates Oregon Health Codes after 8pm.

That's not helping.  I need a specific time the party starts.

If you're here before 2pm you get chores, but at the same time don't think you can show up at 6pm and cut in line for some pig.

Can I bring a friend?  Their friends? Their pets?

Sure!  Pigsgiving attendance rules, like the event itself, are played fast and loose.  The single constant in all these years is we have never run out of pig.  Even with the smaller pig this year, you'll probably get some leftovers to take home.

I think this one is the worst and will definitely come up years from now in therapy.  Pleasant dreams!
